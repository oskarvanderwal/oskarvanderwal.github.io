Chapter 4 — Missing Table

Chapter 4 presents a pilot study of scenario-grounded bias evaluation for examining racial bias in language models using realistic clinical documentation. We introduce the Patient Notes dataset — 51 synthetic but clinically plausible patient notes written by medical students, each paired with a counterfactual version that explicitly identifies the patient as Black or African American. Ten language models are evaluated through expert annotation of free-text responses to common clinical decision-support questions.

The table below was accidentally omitted from the published thesis, but is referenced in Section 4.5.3 (Validation). It reports Kendall's Tau rank correlations between how models are ranked by their number of inappropriate, error, and appropriate responses on the Patient Notes evaluation, and how they rank on three external benchmarks: MedQA accuracy, SHADR demographic robustness (accuracy and bias score), and RealToxicityPrompts. A positive correlation means models that perform better on a benchmark also tend to produce fewer inappropriate (or more appropriate) responses on Patient Notes.

The Intervention/Baseline row captures the ratio of inappropriate responses in the intervention subset (Black/African American identifier present) relative to the baseline, providing a direct measure of sensitivity to racial identification. Cells are colour-coded from yellow (weaker or negative correlation) to green (stronger positive correlation).
